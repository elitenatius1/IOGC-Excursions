Missouri Botanical Garden Overview
📍 Overview
The Missouri Botanical Garden is one of the oldest and most renowned botanical gardens in the United States. Saints will enjoy peaceful walking paths, striking conservatories, seasonal blooms, and beautifully curated landscapes — all in the heart of the city.
Helpful distance estimates:
  • From the Renaissance St. Louis Airport Hotel: roughly a 25–30 minute drive to the Missouri Botanical Garden, depending on traffic.
  • To downtown excursions: Gateway Arch, Old Courthouse, and the Riverfront are roughly 15–20 minutes from the Garden by car.
  • To Union Station: Union Station attractions are roughly 10–15 minutes from the Garden if Saints wish to combine both excursions.
The Missouri Botanical Garden pairs well with a visit to nearby Tower Grove Park, or Saints can head downtown after their visit to experience the Gateway Arch and Riverfront.
Anchor Attraction
Missouri Botanical Garden ← click to explore
Founded in 1859, the Missouri Botanical Garden spans 79 acres and features world-class collections including the iconic Climatron® geodesic dome conservatory, a stunning Japanese garden, rose gardens, and seasonal floral displays. It is a National Historic Landmark and one of the top botanical gardens in the world.

Garden Highlights
🌿
Climatron® Conservatory ← click to explore
A striking geodesic dome greenhouse housing a lush tropical rainforest environment with exotic plants, cascading waterfalls, and vibrant flowers. One of the most iconic structures in St. Louis.

Included with garden admission.
⛩️
Seiwa-en Japanese Garden ← click to explore
One of the largest traditional Japanese gardens in North America. Stroll around a serene central lake surrounded by stone lanterns, bridges, and carefully manicured landscapes.

Included with garden admission.
🌹
Rose Garden ← click to explore
A beautifully maintained formal garden featuring hundreds of rose varieties in full bloom throughout the season. A perfect spot for photos and a peaceful walk.

Included with garden admission.
🛍️
Garden Gate Shop ← click to explore
Browse a curated selection of garden-inspired gifts, books, plants, and souvenirs. A great stop before heading out.

Hours: Open during regular garden hours.

Driving directions
From the Missouri Botanical Garden, head north on Tower Grove Avenue and merge onto I-44 West toward downtown St. Louis.
